Financial Document Collection Checklist

King County Superior Court requires specific financial documents under Local Family Law Rule 10 (LFLR 10) for any hearing involving child support, spousal maintenance, or other financial issues. This checklist guides you through collecting every required document.

Check each item as you locate it. Bring everything to your attorney or upload it through the firm’s secure portal. Some sections are conditional — read the toggle question at the top of each to determine whether it applies to you.

⚠ Redaction Required Before Filing

Before providing documents to your attorney for court filing, you must black out: (1) Social Security numbers   (2) Driver’s license numbers   (3) Telephone numbers   (4) Children’s dates of birth   (5) All but the last four digits of any account number. Flag these when handing over documents.

✓ All items complete!

You’ve checked every applicable item. Contact your attorney to arrange delivery of your documents.

1
Financial Declaration (Court Form)
0 / 1 ▼

The Financial Declaration is the required court form that summarizes your income, monthly expenses, assets, debts, and available cash. All supporting documents in this checklist are exhibits to this form. Your attorney will provide the current version of the form.

Signed Financial Declaration (WPF DR 01.0550 or current equivalent form)
Complete every section before signing. Write “N/A” on any section that does not apply rather than leaving it blank.
2
Pay Stubs — Last 6 Months
0 / 3 ▼

Collect all pay stubs or equivalent income records for every pay period in the past six months, measured back from the date of your hearing or filing. All income sources must be documented.

Pay stubs from all jobs and employers, covering every pay period in the past six months
Include all employers. Do not skip any period, even partial ones.
If you do not receive pay stubs: bank records, invoices, or contracts showing all income and deductions
Required for self-employed individuals, contractors, gig workers, and cash-paid workers. Check this if not applicable to you.
If you receive government benefits: award letters or benefit statements (Social Security, SSDI/SSI, unemployment, workers’ compensation, VA benefits)
All income sources must be documented regardless of source. Check this if not applicable to you.
3
Personal Tax Returns — Prior Two Years
0 / 6 ▼

Provide complete federal tax returns for each of the two most recent calendar years — not a summary, transcript, or preview. Every schedule, W-2, and 1099 must be included.

Most Recent Tax Year
Federal Form 1040 for the most recent filed year (complete return as filed)
Must be the full return, not a draft or tax software summary screen.
All Schedules filed with that return (A, B, C, D, E, F, SE, 1, 2, 3, and any others)
Include every schedule even if it shows a loss, zero, or a single entry.
All W-2 and 1099 forms for the most recent tax year (from every employer and payer)
Collect every W-2 and every 1099 type you received (1099-INT, 1099-DIV, 1099-NEC, 1099-R, 1099-SSA, 1099-G, etc.).
Prior Tax Year
Federal Form 1040 for the prior year (complete return as filed)
All Schedules filed with the prior year return
All W-2 and 1099 forms for the prior tax year
Also include any state tax returns if you lived or worked in a state with an income tax either year (Washington has none).
4
Business Entity Tax Returns
N/A ▼

Required only if you own 5% or more of any corporation, partnership, LLC, S-Corp, or other entity that files its own tax return. Answer the question below to determine whether this section applies.

Complete business tax return(s) for each entity for the prior two years (Form 1120, 1120-S, 1065, or equivalent)
Must include all schedules, attachments, and K-1 forms. One complete return per entity, per year.
List of all business entities in which you hold a 5% or greater ownership interest (name, type, ownership %)
Entity type examples: S-Corp, C-Corp, LLC, partnership, LLP.
5
Financial Account Statements — Last 6 Months
0 / 7 ▼

Collect statements for every financial account in which you have or had any interest during the past six months. “Financial institutions” is defined broadly — review every category below.

Checking and savings account statements from all banks and credit unions
Include personal, joint, and any account over which you have signing authority.
Investment and brokerage account statements (stocks, bonds, ETFs, mutual funds)
Fidelity, Schwab, Vanguard, Merrill Lynch, Edward Jones, and similar platforms.
Retirement account statements (401(k), 403(b), IRA, Roth IRA, SEP-IRA, SIMPLE IRA, pension plan)
Monthly or quarterly statements covering the full six-month period.
Money market funds, certificates of deposit (CDs), and Treasury bills
Include CDs even if they have not yet matured.
Health Savings Account (HSA) and Flexible Spending Account (FSA) statements
Include if either party holds one of these accounts. Check if not applicable.
Cryptocurrency exchange and digital wallet statements (Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, Gemini, or similar)
Include any account holding or transacting in cryptocurrency. Check if not applicable.
Statements for accounts closed within the past six months
Include the final statement and, if available, the closure confirmation. Check if not applicable.
6
Non-Taxable Income and Benefits
N/A ▼

Required only if you receive (or have received in the past two years) income or benefits that do not appear on your tax return. Common examples: trust distributions, regular gifts, barter income, or certain settlement proceeds.

Trust distributions: trustee statements, trust accountings, or distribution letters for the prior two years
Include the trust name, trustee contact, and your relationship to the trust.
Gifts of money or property: written documentation of the source, amount, and date of each gift
Recurring or large one-time gifts must be documented. Family gifts count.
Barter income: records of the fair market value of goods or services received in exchange for your goods or services
Workers’ compensation or personal injury settlement proceeds not reflected in your tax return
Provide award letters, settlement agreements, or payment records.
Regular payments received from another person (other than a spouse) not reported as income
Document the source, amount, and frequency.
7
Child Support Worksheets
N/A ▼

Required only if either party is asking the Court to order, modify, or address child support — or to order payment of a child’s expenses (tuition, childcare, medical costs, etc.).

Completed Washington State Child Support Worksheets (WSCSS Worksheets)
Both parties must file. Ask your attorney whether you need to prepare your own or whether they will prepare from your income data.
Current childcare cost documentation: invoices, enrollment letters, or provider statements (past 6 months)
Children’s health insurance premium documentation (showing the portion attributable to the children, not the full family premium)
Documentation of extraordinary medical, educational, or special needs expenses for the children (receipts, invoices, or statements for the past 6 months)
8
Check Registers — If Requested
0 / 1 ▼

Check registers are not automatically required. However, if the other party requests them, you must produce them within 14 days. Keep your records accessible and organized now so you can meet that deadline.

14-Day Deadline

The production deadline runs from the date of the other party’s written request — not from when you discover the request. Tell your attorney immediately upon receiving any such request.

Physical checkbook register or printed/downloaded transaction history for all checking accounts with activity in the past six months
Retain these even if no request has been made. Check this box once you have confirmed your records are organized and accessible.

Filing Instructions
▼
Sealed Financial Source Documents

Tax returns, pay stubs, bank statements, and other financial institution statements must not be attached directly to the Financial Declaration. They are submitted separately to the Clerk under a cover sheet captioned “Sealed Financial Source Documents.” Once filed under seal, only a party to the case or their attorney may access these documents from the court file without a separate court order.

Redact Before Filing — Final Checklist

Confirm with your attorney that the following have been blacked out on every document before filing:

  • Social Security numbers
  • Driver’s license numbers
  • Telephone numbers
  • Children’s dates of birth
  • All but the last four digits of any account number
